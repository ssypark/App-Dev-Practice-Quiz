Topic 5: Integumentary System Quiz (Part 2)

1. True or False: Melanin is the only pigment contributing to skin color.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Skin color is influenced by melanin, carotene, and hemoglobin.

2. Which of the following layers is NOT part of the epidermis?

  • A) Stratum corneum
  • B) Stratum lucidum
  • C) Stratum granulosum
  • D) Papillary layer
Answer: D) Papillary layer
Explanation: The papillary layer is part of the dermis, not the epidermis.

3. What is the primary role of Langerhans cells in the epidermis?

  • A) Producing keratin
  • B) Regulating water loss
  • C) Acting as immune cells
  • D) Producing melanin
Answer: C) Acting as immune cells
Explanation: Langerhans cells are antigen-presenting cells that help initiate the immune response in the skin.

4. True or False: Sebaceous glands are found on both thick and thin skin.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Sebaceous glands are found in thin skin but are absent in thick skin like the palms and soles.

5. Which of the following is a function of the skin?

  • A) Protection against UV radiation
  • B) Regulation of body temperature
  • C) Sensory perception
  • D) All of the above
Answer: D) All of the above
Explanation: The skin serves multiple functions, including protection, temperature regulation, and sensory input.

6. True or False: Hair follicles are located in the dermis.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Hair follicles are embedded in the dermis, where they are anchored and supported.

7. What type of sweat gland becomes active during puberty and is associated with hair follicles?

  • A) Eccrine glands
  • B) Apocrine glands
  • C) Sebaceous glands
  • D) Ceruminous glands
Answer: B) Apocrine glands
Explanation: Apocrine glands become active during puberty and are located in areas like the armpits and groin, secreting sweat associated with hair follicles.

8. True or False: The stratum lucidum is present in both thick and thin skin.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: The stratum lucidum is only present in thick skin, such as the palms of the hands and soles of the feet.

9. Which of the following layers of the epidermis is responsible for cell division and new cell production?

  • A) Stratum basale
  • B) Stratum spinosum
  • C) Stratum granulosum
  • D) Stratum corneum
Answer: A) Stratum basale
Explanation: The stratum basale is the deepest layer of the epidermis, where cell division occurs to replace dead skin cells.

10. What is the role of ceruminous glands in the skin?

  • A) Producing sweat
  • B) Producing earwax
  • C) Lubricating the skin
  • D) Producing melanin
Answer: B) Producing earwax
Explanation: Ceruminous glands, located in the ear canal, produce earwax (cerumen) to protect and lubricate the ear.

11. True or False: The reticular region of the dermis provides strength and elasticity to the skin.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The reticular region of the dermis contains dense irregular connective tissue, providing strength and elasticity to the skin.

12. What is the primary function of the hypodermis?

  • A) Insulation and cushioning
  • B) Producing sweat
  • C) Synthesizing vitamin D
  • D) Protecting the hair follicles
Answer: A) Insulation and cushioning
Explanation: The hypodermis, also known as the subcutaneous layer, provides insulation, cushioning, and energy storage.

13. Which pigment is responsible for the yellow-orange coloration of the skin?

  • A) Melanin
  • B) Carotene
  • C) Hemoglobin
  • D) Collagen
Answer: B) Carotene
Explanation: Carotene is a yellow-orange pigment stored in the skin that contributes to its coloration.

14. True or False: Hair grows from the epidermis and contains living cells throughout its structure.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Hair originates in the dermis, and only the cells in the hair bulb are living; the rest of the hair shaft is made of keratinized dead cells.

15. What is the role of Merkel cells in the epidermis?

  • A) Producing keratin
  • B) Providing sensory information
  • C) Producing melanin
  • D) Regulating sweat production
Answer: B) Providing sensory information
Explanation: Merkel cells are sensory receptors in the epidermis, involved in the detection of touch.

16. What is the name of the structure that produces hair?

  • A) Sebaceous gland
  • B) Hair bulb
  • C) Hair shaft
  • D) Hair matrix
Answer: B) Hair bulb
Explanation: The hair bulb is the base of the hair follicle where hair cells divide and produce the hair shaft.

17. True or False: The arrector pili muscle is responsible for raising hair and causing "goosebumps."

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The arrector pili muscle contracts to raise hair, which can occur during cold temperatures or emotional responses.

18. Which of the following layers of the skin contains blood vessels?

  • A) Epidermis
  • B) Dermis
  • C) Stratum corneum
  • D) Stratum lucidum
Answer: B) Dermis
Explanation: The dermis is the layer of the skin that contains blood vessels, nerves, and other structures that support the epidermis.

19. What type of sweat gland is primarily involved in thermoregulation?

  • A) Apocrine glands
  • B) Eccrine glands
  • C) Sebaceous glands
  • D) Ceruminous glands
Answer: B) Eccrine glands
Explanation: Eccrine glands secrete sweat directly onto the skin surface to help cool the body during thermoregulation.

20. True or False: Melanin provides protection against UV radiation.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Melanin absorbs and disperses UV radiation, protecting skin cells from DNA damage.

21. What is the thickest layer of the dermis?

  • A) Papillary layer
  • B) Reticular layer
  • C) Epidermal layer
  • D) Hypodermis
Answer: B) Reticular layer
Explanation: The reticular layer of the dermis is thicker than the papillary layer and provides skin strength and elasticity.

22. True or False: The hypodermis is a part of the skin.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: The hypodermis, also called the subcutaneous layer, is not technically part of the skin but connects the skin to underlying tissues.

23. What type of connective tissue primarily makes up the dermis?

  • A) Loose connective tissue
  • B) Dense irregular connective tissue
  • C) Adipose tissue
  • D) Cartilage
Answer: B) Dense irregular connective tissue
Explanation: The dermis is composed of dense irregular connective tissue, providing strength and flexibility to the skin.

24. What layer of the skin is involved in vitamin D synthesis?

  • A) Epidermis
  • B) Dermis
  • C) Hypodermis
  • D) Stratum corneum
Answer: A) Epidermis
Explanation: Vitamin D synthesis begins in the epidermis when skin is exposed to UV light.

25. True or False: The stratum corneum is composed entirely of dead cells.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The stratum corneum, the outermost layer of the epidermis, is composed entirely of keratinized dead cells that provide a protective barrier.

26. What is the primary function of sebaceous glands?

  • A) Producing sweat
  • B) Producing sebum
  • C) Synthesizing keratin
  • D) Producing melanin
Answer: B) Producing sebum
Explanation: Sebaceous glands secrete sebum, an oily substance that lubricates the skin and hair.

27. True or False: Collagen fibers in the dermis provide elasticity to the skin.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Collagen fibers provide strength to the dermis, while elastic fibers are responsible for elasticity.

28. What is the name of the connective tissue that surrounds each hair follicle?

  • A) Papillary dermis
  • B) Reticular dermis
  • C) Dermal root sheath
  • D) Hypodermis
Answer: C) Dermal root sheath
Explanation: The dermal root sheath surrounds the hair follicle and provides it with structural support.

29. True or False: The papillary layer of the dermis contains capillaries and tactile receptors.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The papillary layer contains capillaries, tactile receptors, and other structures that nourish the epidermis and detect touch.

30. Which layer of the epidermis is responsible for waterproofing the skin?

  • A) Stratum corneum
  • B) Stratum lucidum
  • C) Stratum granulosum
  • D) Stratum spinosum
Answer: C) Stratum granulosum
Explanation: The stratum granulosum contains keratohyalin granules, which help in waterproofing the skin.
