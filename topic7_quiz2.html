Topic 7: Muscular Tissue Quiz

1. What are the three types of muscle tissue in the human body?

  • A) Skeletal, smooth, and connective
  • B) Skeletal, cardiac, and smooth
  • C) Voluntary, involuntary, and striated
  • D) Skeletal, voluntary, and involuntary
Answer: B) Skeletal, cardiac, and smooth
Explanation: The three types of muscle tissue are skeletal (voluntary), cardiac (heart), and smooth (involuntary).

2. True or False: Skeletal muscle is involuntary and non-striated.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Skeletal muscle is voluntary and striated, controlled consciously to produce movement.

3. Which of the following describes the main function of cardiac muscle?

  • A) Moving bones and joints
  • B) Contracting blood vessels
  • C) Pumping blood through the heart
  • D) Digesting food in the stomach
Answer: C) Pumping blood through the heart
Explanation: Cardiac muscle is responsible for the rhythmic contractions of the heart, pumping blood to the body.

4. True or False: Smooth muscle is found in the walls of hollow organs like the stomach and intestines.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Smooth muscle lines the walls of hollow organs and is responsible for involuntary movements like peristalsis.

5. What is the basic structural and functional unit of a skeletal muscle fiber?

  • A) Myofibril
  • B) Sarcomere
  • C) Actin
  • D) Myosin
Answer: B) Sarcomere
Explanation: The sarcomere is the functional unit of skeletal muscle, composed of actin and myosin filaments that slide to produce contraction.

6. True or False: Cardiac muscle fibers are multinucleated.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Cardiac muscle fibers typically have a single nucleus and are interconnected by intercalated discs.

7. What protein binds to calcium ions during muscle contraction?

  • A) Tropomyosin
  • B) Myosin
  • C) Troponin
  • D) Actin
Answer: C) Troponin
Explanation: Troponin binds calcium ions, causing a conformational change that moves tropomyosin and allows actin-myosin interaction during contraction.

8. True or False: ATP is required for both the contraction and relaxation of muscle fibers.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: ATP is necessary for the detachment of myosin heads from actin and the reuptake of calcium ions into the sarcoplasmic reticulum during relaxation.

9. Which of the following structures stores calcium ions in a muscle fiber?

  • A) T-tubules
  • B) Sarcoplasmic reticulum
  • C) Myofibrils
  • D) Sarcolemma
Answer: B) Sarcoplasmic reticulum
Explanation: The sarcoplasmic reticulum stores calcium ions and releases them during muscle contraction.

10. True or False: Smooth muscle contracts more slowly than skeletal muscle but can sustain contractions for a longer time.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Smooth muscle contraction is slower than skeletal muscle contraction but is energy-efficient and can be sustained for long periods.

11. What is the role of intercalated discs in cardiac muscle?

  • A) To store calcium
  • B) To generate ATP
  • C) To allow synchronized contraction
  • D) To attach muscles to bones
Answer: C) To allow synchronized contraction
Explanation: Intercalated discs in cardiac muscle connect cells electrically and mechanically, enabling coordinated contraction.

12. What is the name of the connective tissue layer that surrounds an entire muscle?

  • A) Perimysium
  • B) Epimysium
  • C) Endomysium
  • D) Sarcolemma
Answer: B) Epimysium
Explanation: The epimysium is the outermost layer of connective tissue that encases an entire muscle.

13. True or False: Myosin heads bind to actin filaments to form cross-bridges during muscle contraction.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Myosin heads attach to actin filaments to form cross-bridges, enabling the sliding filament mechanism of muscle contraction.

14. Which neurotransmitter is primarily involved in stimulating skeletal muscle contraction?

  • A) Dopamine
  • B) Acetylcholine
  • C) Serotonin
  • D) Norepinephrine
Answer: B) Acetylcholine
Explanation: Acetylcholine is released at the neuromuscular junction, triggering muscle fiber depolarization and contraction.

15. True or False: Sarcomeres shorten during muscle contraction due to the sliding of thick and thin filaments past each other.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The sliding filament mechanism shortens sarcomeres as myosin heads pull actin filaments inward during contraction.

16. What is the role of T-tubules in muscle contraction?

  • A) Storing calcium ions
  • B) Conducting electrical impulses into muscle fibers
  • C) Synthesizing ATP
  • D) Binding to myosin
Answer: B) Conducting electrical impulses into muscle fibers
Explanation: T-tubules conduct action potentials deep into the muscle fiber, triggering calcium release from the sarcoplasmic reticulum.

17. True or False: Smooth muscle lacks sarcomeres and appears non-striated under a microscope.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Smooth muscle cells do not have sarcomeres, giving them a non-striated appearance under a microscope.

18. Which type of muscle tissue is capable of spontaneous contraction?

  • A) Skeletal
  • B) Cardiac
  • C) Smooth
  • D) Both B and C
Answer: D) Both B and C
Explanation: Both cardiac and smooth muscle tissues can contract spontaneously due to pacemaker cells.

19. What molecule is required to release myosin heads from actin during relaxation?

  • A) ATP
  • B) ADP
  • C) Calcium
  • D) Tropomyosin
Answer: A) ATP
Explanation: ATP binds to myosin heads, causing them to release from actin and allowing relaxation.

20. True or False: The H zone in a sarcomere shortens during muscle contraction.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The H zone, which contains only thick filaments, shortens as the thin filaments slide inward during contraction.

21. Which structure anchors the thin filaments in a sarcomere?

  • A) Z disc
  • B) M line
  • C) H zone
  • D) A band
Answer: A) Z disc
Explanation: The Z disc anchors the thin filaments and marks the boundary of each sarcomere.

22. True or False: Muscle tone refers to the sustained contraction of some muscle fibers, even at rest.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Muscle tone is the result of continuous, low-level contraction of muscle fibers, helping maintain posture and readiness for action.

23. What type of contraction occurs when muscle tension increases, but the muscle does not change length?

  • A) Isotonic
  • B) Isometric
  • C) Concentric
  • D) Eccentric
Answer: B) Isometric
Explanation: During isometric contraction, the muscle generates tension without changing length, such as when holding an object steady.

24. Which type of muscle contraction shortens the muscle as it generates tension?

  • A) Isotonic
  • B) Concentric
  • C) Eccentric
  • D) Isometric
Answer: B) Concentric
Explanation: Concentric contractions shorten the muscle as it produces force, such as lifting a weight.

25. True or False: Fast glycolytic fibers produce powerful contractions but fatigue quickly.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Fast glycolytic fibers generate rapid, powerful contractions but rely on anaerobic metabolism and fatigue quickly.

26. Which type of muscle fiber is best suited for endurance activities?

  • A) Slow oxidative fibers
  • B) Fast oxidative fibers
  • C) Fast glycolytic fibers
  • D) Smooth muscle fibers
Answer: A) Slow oxidative fibers
Explanation: Slow oxidative fibers are fatigue-resistant and use aerobic metabolism, making them ideal for endurance activities like marathon running.

27. True or False: The A band in a sarcomere remains the same length during muscle contraction.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The A band, which contains the entire length of thick filaments, remains unchanged during contraction.

28. What is the primary source of energy for muscle contractions during short, intense activities?

  • A) Fatty acids
  • B) Glucose
  • C) Creatine phosphate
  • D) Glycogen
Answer: C) Creatine phosphate
Explanation: Creatine phosphate donates a phosphate group to ADP, rapidly regenerating ATP during short, high-intensity activities.

29. Which enzyme breaks down acetylcholine at the neuromuscular junction?

  • A) Acetylcholinesterase
  • B) ATPase
  • C) Myokinase
  • D) Choline acetyltransferase
Answer: A) Acetylcholinesterase
Explanation: Acetylcholinesterase breaks down acetylcholine, terminating the signal at the neuromuscular junction.

30. True or False: Hypertrophy is an increase in the number of muscle fibers.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Hypertrophy is an increase in the size of muscle fibers, not their number, typically due to resistance training.
