Topic 8: Nervous Tissue Quiz

1. What are the two main types of cells in nervous tissue?

  • A) Neurons and neuroglia
  • B) Axons and dendrites
  • C) Myelin and synapses
  • D) Nerves and ganglia
Answer: A) Neurons and neuroglia
Explanation: Nervous tissue consists of neurons, which transmit signals, and neuroglia, which support and protect neurons.

2. True or False: Neurons can divide and regenerate in all parts of the nervous system.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Most neurons in the central nervous system (CNS) cannot regenerate, but neurons in the peripheral nervous system (PNS) may regenerate under certain conditions.

3. Which part of a neuron receives incoming signals?

  • A) Axon
  • B) Soma
  • C) Dendrites
  • D) Axon terminal
Answer: C) Dendrites
Explanation: Dendrites are the branched extensions of a neuron that receive signals from other neurons.

4. True or False: Myelin sheaths increase the speed of nerve impulse conduction.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Myelin sheaths insulate axons, allowing nerve impulses to travel faster through saltatory conduction.

5. What is the functional junction between two neurons called?

  • A) Synapse
  • B) Gap junction
  • C) Axon hillock
  • D) Dendritic spine
Answer: A) Synapse
Explanation: A synapse is the point of communication between two neurons or a neuron and a target cell.

6. Which type of neuroglia produces cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)?

  • A) Astrocytes
  • B) Ependymal cells
  • C) Microglia
  • D) Oligodendrocytes
Answer: B) Ependymal cells
Explanation: Ependymal cells line the brain's ventricles and the central canal of the spinal cord, producing and circulating cerebrospinal fluid.

7. Which type of neuroglia forms the myelin sheath in the central nervous system (CNS)?

  • A) Schwann cells
  • B) Astrocytes
  • C) Microglia
  • D) Oligodendrocytes
Answer: D) Oligodendrocytes
Explanation: Oligodendrocytes produce the myelin sheath around axons in the CNS, aiding in faster signal transmission.

8. True or False: Schwann cells are responsible for myelination in the peripheral nervous system (PNS).

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Schwann cells myelinate axons in the PNS, allowing efficient signal conduction.

9. What is the name of the gaps between segments of myelin sheath along an axon?

  • A) Nodes of Ranvier
  • B) Axon terminals
  • C) Synaptic clefts
  • D) Dendritic spines
Answer: A) Nodes of Ranvier
Explanation: The Nodes of Ranvier are unmyelinated gaps that facilitate saltatory conduction, speeding up nerve impulse transmission.

10. True or False: The sodium-potassium pump actively transports sodium ions into the cell and potassium ions out of the cell.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: The sodium-potassium pump actively transports sodium ions out of the cell and potassium ions into the cell to maintain the resting membrane potential.

11. What type of potential is generated when a neuron is sufficiently stimulated to reach its threshold?

  • A) Resting potential
  • B) Graded potential
  • C) Action potential
  • D) Postsynaptic potential
Answer: C) Action potential
Explanation: When a neuron reaches its threshold, an action potential is generated, propagating the signal along the axon.

12. True or False: Excitatory neurotransmitters cause hyperpolarization of the postsynaptic membrane.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Excitatory neurotransmitters cause depolarization of the postsynaptic membrane, bringing it closer to the threshold for an action potential.

13. Which ion is primarily responsible for depolarization during an action potential?

  • A) Sodium (Na+)
  • B) Potassium (K+)
  • C) Calcium (Ca2+)
  • D) Chloride (Cl-)
Answer: A) Sodium (Na+)
Explanation: Depolarization occurs as sodium ions flow into the cell through voltage-gated sodium channels, making the membrane potential more positive.

14. True or False: The all-or-none principle states that a neuron fires an action potential only if the stimulus reaches the threshold level.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: According to the all-or-none principle, an action potential is triggered only if the stimulus exceeds the threshold level.

15. What is the role of calcium ions (Ca2+) at the synapse?

  • A) Triggering the release of neurotransmitters
  • B) Initiating depolarization
  • C) Inhibiting postsynaptic neurons
  • D) Maintaining resting potential
Answer: A) Triggering the release of neurotransmitters
Explanation: Calcium ions enter the presynaptic terminal, triggering the release of neurotransmitters into the synaptic cleft.

16. Which structure in a neuron is responsible for transmitting signals to other cells?

  • A) Dendrites
  • B) Axon
  • C) Soma
  • D) Nissl bodies
Answer: B) Axon
Explanation: The axon transmits signals from the neuron to other cells, including other neurons, muscles, or glands.

17. True or False: The refractory period ensures that an action potential travels in only one direction along an axon.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The refractory period prevents backward propagation of the action potential, ensuring it travels in one direction along the axon.

18. Which type of neurotransmitter is associated with inhibitory effects?

  • A) Glutamate
  • B) Dopamine
  • C) GABA
  • D) Acetylcholine
Answer: C) GABA
Explanation: Gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) is the primary inhibitory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system.

19. True or False: The blood-brain barrier is primarily maintained by astrocytes.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Astrocytes help maintain the blood-brain barrier by regulating substances that enter the brain's extracellular fluid.

20. Which region of the neuron contains the nucleus?

  • A) Axon
  • B) Soma
  • C) Dendrites
  • D) Synaptic cleft
Answer: B) Soma
Explanation: The soma, or cell body, contains the nucleus and organelles responsible for the neuron's metabolic activities.

21. What type of conduction occurs in myelinated axons?

  • A) Continuous conduction
  • B) Saltatory conduction
  • C) Passive conduction
  • D) Axoplasmic conduction
Answer: B) Saltatory conduction
Explanation: In saltatory conduction, the action potential jumps between Nodes of Ranvier, significantly increasing conduction speed.

22. True or False: Sensory neurons carry signals from the central nervous system to effectors such as muscles.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Sensory neurons carry signals from sensory receptors to the central nervous system, not from the CNS to effectors.

23. Which part of the nervous system is responsible for involuntary control of bodily functions?

  • A) Somatic nervous system
  • B) Autonomic nervous system
  • C) Peripheral nervous system
  • D) Central nervous system
Answer: B) Autonomic nervous system
Explanation: The autonomic nervous system regulates involuntary functions such as heart rate, digestion, and respiratory rate.

24. True or False: Oligodendrocytes myelinate multiple axons, while Schwann cells myelinate only one axon each.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Oligodendrocytes can myelinate multiple axons in the CNS, while Schwann cells typically myelinate a single axon in the PNS.

25. Which ion is responsible for repolarization of the neuron membrane during an action potential?

  • A) Sodium (Na+)
  • B) Potassium (K+)
  • C) Calcium (Ca2+)
  • D) Chloride (Cl-)
Answer: B) Potassium (K+)
Explanation: During repolarization, potassium ions flow out of the cell, restoring the resting membrane potential.

26. True or False: The peripheral nervous system includes the brain and spinal cord.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: The brain and spinal cord are part of the central nervous system (CNS), not the peripheral nervous system (PNS).

27. What is the primary function of microglia in the nervous system?

  • A) Producing myelin
  • B) Phagocytosis of debris and pathogens
  • C) Maintaining the blood-brain barrier
  • D) Transmitting nerve impulses
Answer: B) Phagocytosis of debris and pathogens
Explanation: Microglia act as immune cells in the CNS, engulfing debris and protecting against pathogens.

28. Which division of the autonomic nervous system is responsible for the "fight or flight" response?

  • A) Parasympathetic division
  • B) Sympathetic division
  • C) Enteric division
  • D) Central division
Answer: B) Sympathetic division
Explanation: The sympathetic division prepares the body for "fight or flight" by increasing heart rate, dilating airways, and mobilizing energy.

29. True or False: The enteric nervous system is a subdivision of the autonomic nervous system.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The enteric nervous system, which controls gastrointestinal function, is a subdivision of the autonomic nervous system.

30. Which ion channels open to initiate depolarization during an action potential?

  • A) Voltage-gated potassium channels
  • B) Voltage-gated sodium channels
  • C) Ligand-gated chloride channels
  • D) Calcium channels
Answer: B) Voltage-gated sodium channels
Explanation: Voltage-gated sodium channels open during depolarization, allowing sodium ions to flow into the neuron.
