Topic 3: Cellular Level of Organization Quiz

1. What is the primary function of the plasma membrane?

  • A) Storing genetic information
  • B) Controlling what enters and exits the cell
  • C) Synthesizing proteins
  • D) Generating ATP
Answer: B) Controlling what enters and exits the cell
Explanation: The plasma membrane acts as a selective barrier, regulating the passage of substances in and out of the cell.

2. True or False: The nucleus is the control center of the cell.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The nucleus contains genetic material (DNA) and directs cellular activities, functioning as the cell's control center.

3. Which of the following are components of the cytoskeleton? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Microtubules
  • B) Intermediate filaments
  • C) Actin filaments
  • D) Lysosomes
Answer: A, B, C
Explanation: The cytoskeleton is made of microtubules, intermediate filaments, and actin filaments, providing structure and support. Lysosomes are not part of the cytoskeleton.

4. What is the role of mitochondria in the cell?

  • A) Synthesizing proteins
  • B) Producing energy in the form of ATP
  • C) Breaking down waste materials
  • D) Packaging and transporting proteins
Answer: B) Producing energy in the form of ATP
Explanation: Mitochondria are known as the "powerhouses" of the cell because they produce ATP through cellular respiration.

5. True or False: Ribosomes are responsible for protein synthesis.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Ribosomes are cellular structures that assemble amino acids into proteins based on instructions from mRNA.

6. Which organelle modifies, packages, and ships proteins?

  • A) Endoplasmic reticulum
  • B) Golgi apparatus
  • C) Lysosome
  • D) Nucleus
Answer: B) Golgi apparatus
Explanation: The Golgi apparatus modifies, packages, and ships proteins to their destination within or outside the cell.

7. True or False: The rough endoplasmic reticulum (ER) is involved in lipid synthesis.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: The rough ER, studded with ribosomes, synthesizes proteins, while the smooth ER is involved in lipid synthesis and detoxification.

8. Which type of transport requires energy (ATP)?

  • A) Simple diffusion
  • B) Osmosis
  • C) Facilitated diffusion
  • D) Active transport
Answer: D) Active transport
Explanation: Active transport requires energy to move substances against their concentration gradient.

9. True or False: The cytoplasm includes all the contents within the plasma membrane except the nucleus.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The cytoplasm contains the cytosol and organelles, excluding the nucleus.

10. Which of the following are passive transport mechanisms? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Simple diffusion
  • B) Facilitated diffusion
  • C) Osmosis
  • D) Endocytosis
Answer: A, B, C
Explanation: Simple diffusion, facilitated diffusion, and osmosis are passive processes that do not require energy. Endocytosis is an active process.

11. What is the primary function of lysosomes?

  • A) Synthesizing proteins
  • B) Producing ATP
  • C) Digesting waste and foreign materials
  • D) Storing genetic information
Answer: C) Digesting waste and foreign materials
Explanation: Lysosomes contain enzymes that break down waste and harmful substances in the cell.

12. True or False: Peroxisomes are involved in the detoxification of harmful substances.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Peroxisomes break down harmful substances like hydrogen peroxide and detoxify compounds such as alcohol.

13. Which organelle is responsible for producing ribosomes?

  • A) Golgi apparatus
  • B) Rough ER
  • C) Nucleolus
  • D) Mitochondria
Answer: C) Nucleolus
Explanation: The nucleolus, located inside the nucleus, is responsible for synthesizing ribosomes.

14. Which of the following are functions of the smooth endoplasmic reticulum? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Synthesizing lipids
  • B) Detoxifying drugs
  • C) Storing calcium
  • D) Producing ATP
Answer: A, B, C
Explanation: The smooth ER synthesizes lipids, detoxifies drugs, and stores calcium. ATP is produced by mitochondria.

15. True or False: Chromosomes are composed of DNA and proteins.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Chromosomes are made of DNA tightly wound around histone proteins, forming chromatin.

16. Which phase of the cell cycle involves DNA replication?

  • A) G1 phase
  • B) S phase
  • C) G2 phase
  • D) M phase
Answer: B) S phase
Explanation: DNA replication occurs during the S phase (synthesis phase) of interphase in the cell cycle.

17. True or False: The mitotic phase includes both mitosis and cytokinesis.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The mitotic phase consists of mitosis (nuclear division) and cytokinesis (cytoplasmic division).

18. What is the primary function of the centrioles during cell division?

  • A) Synthesizing DNA
  • B) Organizing the mitotic spindle
  • C) Producing energy
  • D) Synthesizing proteins
Answer: B) Organizing the mitotic spindle
Explanation: Centrioles help organize the mitotic spindle, which separates chromosomes during cell division.

19. True or False: Osmosis is the diffusion of solutes across a semi-permeable membrane.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Osmosis is the diffusion of water, not solutes, across a semi-permeable membrane.

20. Which organelle is responsible for detoxifying harmful substances in liver cells?

  • A) Golgi apparatus
  • B) Rough ER
  • C) Smooth ER
  • D) Peroxisomes
Answer: C) Smooth ER
Explanation: The smooth ER detoxifies harmful substances such as drugs and alcohol in liver cells.

21. True or False: Mitochondria have their own DNA and ribosomes.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Mitochondria contain their own DNA and ribosomes, enabling them to produce some of their own proteins.

22. Which of the following are characteristics of active transport? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Requires energy
  • B) Moves substances down their concentration gradient
  • C) Uses transport proteins
  • D) Moves substances against their concentration gradient
Answer: A, C, D
Explanation: Active transport requires energy, uses transport proteins, and moves substances against their concentration gradient.

23. True or False: Endocytosis is a process by which cells release substances.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Endocytosis is a process by which cells engulf substances. Exocytosis is the process of releasing substances.

24. What happens to a cell placed in a hypertonic solution?

  • A) It swells
  • B) It shrinks
  • C) It remains unchanged
  • D) It bursts
Answer: B) It shrinks
Explanation: In a hypertonic solution, water moves out of the cell, causing it to shrink.

25. Which phase of mitosis involves the chromosomes aligning at the cell's equator?

  • A) Prophase
  • B) Metaphase
  • C) Anaphase
  • D) Telophase
Answer: B) Metaphase
Explanation: During metaphase, chromosomes align along the metaphase plate at the cell's equator.

26. True or False: Phagocytosis is a type of endocytosis.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Phagocytosis, or "cell eating," is a form of endocytosis where the cell engulfs large particles.

27. Which organelle is most abundant in cells requiring high energy, such as muscle cells?

  • A) Golgi apparatus
  • B) Lysosomes
  • C) Mitochondria
  • D) Nucleus
Answer: C) Mitochondria
Explanation: Mitochondria are abundant in energy-demanding cells, such as muscle cells, due to their role in producing ATP.

28. Which process involves the movement of large molecules out of a cell?

  • A) Endocytosis
  • B) Exocytosis
  • C) Osmosis
  • D) Facilitated diffusion
Answer: B) Exocytosis
Explanation: Exocytosis is the process where vesicles fuse with the plasma membrane to release large molecules outside the cell.

29. Which of the following are part of the cytoskeleton? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Microtubules
  • B) Actin filaments
  • C) Centrioles
  • D) Intermediate filaments
Answer: A, B, D
Explanation: Microtubules, actin filaments, and intermediate filaments are components of the cytoskeleton. Centrioles are not part of the cytoskeleton but assist in cell division.

30. True or False: Cells placed in an isotonic solution experience no net movement of water.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: In an isotonic solution, water moves in and out of the cell at equal rates, so there is no net movement of water.
