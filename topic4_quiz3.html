Topic 4: Tissue Level of Organization Quiz (Part 3)

1. Which type of epithelial tissue is specialized for secretion and absorption?

  • A) Simple squamous
  • B) Simple cuboidal
  • C) Stratified squamous
  • D) Transitional
Answer: B) Simple cuboidal
Explanation: Simple cuboidal epithelium is found in glands and ducts, where it is involved in secretion and absorption.

2. True or False: Dense irregular connective tissue has fibers arranged in parallel bundles.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Dense irregular connective tissue has fibers arranged randomly, providing strength in multiple directions.

3. What is the primary function of adipose tissue?

  • A) Structural support
  • B) Protection
  • C) Energy storage
  • D) Transport
Answer: C) Energy storage
Explanation: Adipose tissue stores energy in the form of triglycerides and also provides insulation and cushioning.

4. True or False: Cartilage is a vascular connective tissue.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Cartilage is avascular, meaning it lacks blood vessels and relies on diffusion for nutrient and waste exchange.

5. Which connective tissue type provides structural support and flexibility, found in the external ear?

  • A) Hyaline cartilage
  • B) Elastic cartilage
  • C) Fibrocartilage
  • D) Reticular connective tissue
Answer: B) Elastic cartilage
Explanation: Elastic cartilage provides flexibility and maintains shape, as seen in the external ear and epiglottis.

6. What is the role of gap junctions in epithelial tissue?

  • A) Strengthen attachment between cells
  • B) Allow communication between cells
  • C) Prevent leakage between cells
  • D) Anchor epithelial cells to the basement membrane
Answer: B) Allow communication between cells
Explanation: Gap junctions enable ions and small molecules to pass between cells, facilitating intercellular communication.

7. True or False: Blood is considered a type of connective tissue.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Blood is a connective tissue because it consists of cells suspended in an extracellular matrix (plasma).

8. What type of epithelial tissue lines the urinary bladder and allows stretching?

  • A) Stratified squamous
  • B) Transitional epithelium
  • C) Simple cuboidal
  • D) Pseudostratified columnar
Answer: B) Transitional epithelium
Explanation: Transitional epithelium lines the urinary bladder, allowing it to stretch as it fills with urine.

9. Which type of muscle tissue is involuntary and striated?

  • A) Skeletal muscle
  • B) Cardiac muscle
  • C) Smooth muscle
  • D) None of the above
Answer: B) Cardiac muscle
Explanation: Cardiac muscle is involuntary and striated, found exclusively in the heart.

10. True or False: Epithelial tissue has a high regenerative capacity due to frequent mitosis.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Epithelial tissue regenerates quickly due to its high rate of mitosis, which is necessary to replace cells exposed to damage.

11. What type of connective tissue provides tensile strength and is found in tendons?

  • A) Areolar tissue
  • B) Dense regular connective tissue
  • C) Elastic connective tissue
  • D) Reticular connective tissue
Answer: B) Dense regular connective tissue
Explanation: Dense regular connective tissue provides tensile strength due to its parallel collagen fibers, ideal for tendons.

12. Which type of epithelial tissue appears to be multilayered but is actually a single layer?

  • A) Simple columnar
  • B) Stratified columnar
  • C) Pseudostratified columnar
  • D) Transitional epithelium
Answer: C) Pseudostratified columnar
Explanation: Pseudostratified columnar epithelium appears layered due to nuclei at different levels but is a single layer of cells.

13. What type of cartilage is the most abundant in the body and provides smooth surfaces for joint movement?

  • A) Elastic cartilage
  • B) Fibrocartilage
  • C) Hyaline cartilage
  • D) Reticular cartilage
Answer: C) Hyaline cartilage
Explanation: Hyaline cartilage is the most common type of cartilage, found in joints, the nose, and the trachea.

14. True or False: Reticular connective tissue provides a framework for organs such as the spleen and lymph nodes.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Reticular connective tissue forms a supportive framework for soft organs such as the spleen and lymph nodes.

15. Which epithelial tissue type is specialized for diffusion and filtration?

  • A) Simple squamous
  • B) Stratified squamous
  • C) Simple cuboidal
  • D) Transitional epithelium
Answer: A) Simple squamous
Explanation: Simple squamous epithelium is thin and allows for efficient diffusion and filtration, such as in the lungs and kidneys.

16. What type of gland secretes substances directly into the bloodstream?

  • A) Exocrine gland
  • B) Endocrine gland
  • C) Sebaceous gland
  • D) Ceruminous gland
Answer: B) Endocrine gland
Explanation: Endocrine glands release hormones directly into the bloodstream, regulating various body functions.

17. True or False: Fibrocartilage provides strong support and is resistant to compression.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Fibrocartilage provides strong support and resists compression, found in intervertebral discs and the pubic symphysis.

18. Which junction between epithelial cells prevents substances from passing between cells?

  • A) Desmosomes
  • B) Tight junctions
  • C) Gap junctions
  • D) Hemidesmosomes
Answer: B) Tight junctions
Explanation: Tight junctions create a barrier that prevents the movement of substances between epithelial cells.

19. What is the role of mast cells in connective tissue?

  • A) Producing collagen fibers
  • B) Secreting antibodies
  • C) Initiating inflammatory responses
  • D) Phagocytosis of pathogens
Answer: C) Initiating inflammatory responses
Explanation: Mast cells release histamine and other chemicals that initiate inflammation during immune responses.

20. Which epithelial tissue type is found in areas subject to abrasion, such as the skin?

  • A) Simple cuboidal
  • B) Stratified squamous
  • C) Pseudostratified columnar
  • D) Transitional
Answer: B) Stratified squamous
Explanation: Stratified squamous epithelium provides protection in areas exposed to friction and abrasion, such as the epidermis.

21. True or False: Elastic fibers in connective tissue allow stretching and recoiling of organs like arteries.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Elastic fibers provide flexibility and resilience, enabling tissues like arteries to stretch and return to their original shape.

22. Which tissue type stores calcium and supports the body structurally?

  • A) Cartilage
  • B) Bone tissue
  • C) Adipose tissue
  • D) Areolar tissue
Answer: B) Bone tissue
Explanation: Bone tissue provides structural support, stores calcium, and protects vital organs.

23. True or False: Simple columnar epithelium often contains goblet cells that secrete mucus.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Goblet cells in simple columnar epithelium produce mucus, aiding in lubrication and protection in areas like the digestive tract.

24. Which tissue type forms the stroma of organs such as the liver and spleen?

  • A) Reticular connective tissue
  • B) Dense irregular connective tissue
  • C) Hyaline cartilage
  • D) Elastic cartilage
Answer: A) Reticular connective tissue
Explanation: Reticular connective tissue forms the supportive framework (stroma) for soft organs like the liver and spleen.

25. Which junction provides strong adhesion between cells in tissues subject to mechanical stress?

  • A) Gap junctions
  • B) Tight junctions
  • C) Desmosomes
  • D) Hemidesmosomes
Answer: C) Desmosomes
Explanation: Desmosomes anchor adjacent cells together, providing mechanical stability in tissues like skin and cardiac muscle.

26. True or False: Transitional epithelium lines the stomach and intestines.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Transitional epithelium lines organs that stretch, such as the bladder, not the stomach or intestines.

27. Which specialized tissue type conducts electrical impulses?

  • A) Muscle tissue
  • B) Connective tissue
  • C) Nervous tissue
  • D) Epithelial tissue
Answer: C) Nervous tissue
Explanation: Nervous tissue is specialized for transmitting electrical impulses, coordinating bodily functions.

28. True or False: Areolar connective tissue is the most widely distributed connective tissue in the body.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Areolar connective tissue is abundant throughout the body, providing support and elasticity in various organs and tissues.

29. Which muscle tissue type is striated and voluntary?

  • A) Smooth muscle
  • B) Cardiac muscle
  • C) Skeletal muscle
  • D) None of the above
Answer: C) Skeletal muscle
Explanation: Skeletal muscle is both striated and under voluntary control, responsible for body movement.

30. Which epithelial tissue type lines the respiratory tract and helps trap particles?

  • A) Simple cuboidal
  • B) Stratified squamous
  • C) Pseudostratified columnar
  • D) Transitional
Answer: C) Pseudostratified columnar
Explanation: Pseudostratified columnar epithelium in the respiratory tract contains cilia that trap and move particles out of the airways.

31. Which type of connective tissue forms tendons and ligaments?

  • A) Dense regular connective tissue
  • B) Dense irregular connective tissue
  • C) Elastic connective tissue
  • D) Reticular connective tissue
Answer: A) Dense regular connective tissue
Explanation: Dense regular connective tissue is made of parallel collagen fibers, providing tensile strength in tendons and ligaments.

32. True or False: Muscle tissue is classified as epithelial tissue.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Muscle tissue is a separate category of tissue, distinct from epithelial, connective, and nervous tissues.

33. Which type of tissue is avascular and depends on diffusion for nutrients?

  • A) Bone
  • B) Cartilage
  • C) Adipose tissue
  • D) Dense irregular connective tissue
Answer: B) Cartilage
Explanation: Cartilage is avascular and relies on diffusion from surrounding tissues to obtain nutrients and oxygen.

34. What is the function of hemidesmosomes in epithelial tissue?

  • A) Anchor epithelial cells to the basement membrane
  • B) Allow ion exchange between cells
  • C) Create a waterproof barrier
  • D) Strengthen intercellular connections
Answer: A) Anchor epithelial cells to the basement membrane
Explanation: Hemidesmosomes attach epithelial cells to the basement membrane, providing structural stability.

35. True or False: Stratified squamous epithelium is keratinized in the skin.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The outermost layer of stratified squamous epithelium in the skin is keratinized, providing protection and waterproofing.

36. Which connective tissue type cushions organs and provides immune defense?

  • A) Reticular connective tissue
  • B) Areolar connective tissue
  • C) Dense regular connective tissue
  • D) Elastic connective tissue
Answer: B) Areolar connective tissue
Explanation: Areolar tissue cushions and protects organs, contains immune cells, and provides elasticity and support.

37. True or False: Nervous tissue is the primary tissue type responsible for communication within the body.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Nervous tissue transmits electrical signals, enabling communication and coordination of body functions.

38. Which tissue type produces movement and heat in the body?

  • A) Nervous tissue
  • B) Muscle tissue
  • C) Connective tissue
  • D) Epithelial tissue
Answer: B) Muscle tissue
Explanation: Muscle tissue contracts to produce movement and generates heat as a byproduct of metabolism.

39. What is the role of cilia on epithelial cells?

  • A) Absorption of nutrients
  • B) Propelling substances along the surface
  • C) Creating a waterproof barrier
  • D) Secretion of enzymes
Answer: B) Propelling substances along the surface
Explanation: Cilia move mucus and other substances along the surface of epithelial cells, such as in the respiratory tract.

40. True or False: Collagen fibers are elastic and allow tissues to stretch significantly.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Collagen fibers are strong and resistant to stretching, providing structural support and tensile strength.

41. Which connective tissue type prevents bones from rubbing against each other?

  • A) Dense connective tissue
  • B) Hyaline cartilage
  • C) Fibrocartilage
  • D) Elastic cartilage
Answer: B) Hyaline cartilage
Explanation: Hyaline cartilage covers the ends of bones in joints, reducing friction and enabling smooth movement.

42. True or False: Smooth muscle tissue is voluntary and striated.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Smooth muscle tissue is involuntary and non-striated, found in walls of hollow organs.

43. Which type of epithelial tissue lines blood vessels?

  • A) Simple squamous epithelium
  • B) Simple columnar epithelium
  • C) Stratified squamous epithelium
  • D) Transitional epithelium
Answer: A) Simple squamous epithelium
Explanation: Simple squamous epithelium lines blood vessels, allowing efficient exchange of substances.

44. Which type of connective tissue stores fat and insulates the body?

  • A) Areolar tissue
  • B) Adipose tissue
  • C) Reticular tissue
  • D) Dense connective tissue
Answer: B) Adipose tissue
Explanation: Adipose tissue stores energy in the form of fat, provides insulation, and cushions organs.

45. True or False: Blood is considered a connective tissue because it has an extracellular matrix.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Blood is classified as connective tissue because its plasma serves as the extracellular matrix, supporting cellular components like red and white blood cells.

46. Which type of gland releases its products through a duct to the surface?

  • A) Endocrine gland
  • B) Exocrine gland
  • C) Sebaceous gland
  • D) Adrenal gland
Answer: B) Exocrine gland
Explanation: Exocrine glands release substances like sweat, saliva, or digestive enzymes through ducts to surfaces like skin or the digestive tract.

47. True or False: Epithelial tissue is highly vascularized.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Epithelial tissue is avascular, meaning it lacks blood vessels and relies on diffusion from underlying connective tissue for nutrients and oxygen.

48. What type of tissue contains lacunae and provides structural support in areas like the nose and trachea?

  • A) Elastic cartilage
  • B) Hyaline cartilage
  • C) Fibrocartilage
  • D) Reticular tissue
Answer: B) Hyaline cartilage
Explanation: Hyaline cartilage contains lacunae housing chondrocytes and provides support in the nose, trachea, and other structures.

49. True or False: Desmosomes are essential for anchoring epithelial cells to the basement membrane.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Desmosomes anchor adjacent cells to each other, while hemidesmosomes attach epithelial cells to the basement membrane.

50. Which connective tissue contains collagen fibers arranged irregularly to resist stress from multiple directions?

  • A) Dense regular connective tissue
  • B) Dense irregular connective tissue
  • C) Elastic connective tissue
  • D) Reticular connective tissue
Answer: B) Dense irregular connective tissue
Explanation: Dense irregular connective tissue has collagen fibers arranged randomly, providing strength in multiple directions, such as in the dermis.
