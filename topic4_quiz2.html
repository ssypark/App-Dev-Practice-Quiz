Topic 4: Tissue Level of Organization Quiz

1. Which of the following is NOT a major type of tissue?

  • A) Muscular
  • B) Nervous
  • C) Cartilaginous
  • D) Connective
Answer: C) Cartilaginous
Explanation: Cartilage is a type of connective tissue, not a primary tissue type. The four primary tissues are epithelial, connective, muscular, and nervous.

2. True or False: Epithelial tissue is avascular.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Epithelial tissue lacks blood vessels and relies on diffusion from underlying tissues for nutrients.

3. Which connective tissue provides flexible support in the external ear?

  • A) Hyaline Cartilage
  • B) Elastic Cartilage
  • C) Fibrocartilage
  • D) Dense Regular Connective Tissue
Answer: B) Elastic Cartilage
Explanation: Elastic cartilage provides flexibility and maintains the shape of structures like the external ear and epiglottis.

4. Which of the following are functions of connective tissue? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Protection
  • B) Energy Storage
  • C) Absorption
  • D) Support
Answer: A, B, D
Explanation: Connective tissue provides protection (e.g., bone), stores energy (e.g., adipose tissue), and offers structural support (e.g., cartilage and bone).

5. True or False: Skeletal muscle is striated and under voluntary control.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Skeletal muscle is striated and contracts under conscious control to produce movement.

6. What is the main function of nervous tissue?

  • A) Providing structural support
  • B) Conducting electrical impulses
  • C) Storing energy
  • D) Absorbing nutrients
Answer: B) Conducting electrical impulses
Explanation: Nervous tissue consists of neurons that generate and transmit electrical signals to control bodily functions.

7. True or False: Cardiac muscle is striated and contracts voluntarily.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Cardiac muscle is striated but contracts involuntarily, as it is controlled by the autonomic nervous system.

8. Which type of epithelial tissue is best suited for rapid diffusion and filtration?

  • A) Simple Squamous
  • B) Stratified Squamous
  • C) Simple Cuboidal
  • D) Transitional
Answer: A) Simple Squamous
Explanation: Simple squamous epithelium is thin, making it ideal for rapid diffusion and filtration, such as in the alveoli of the lungs.

9. Which of the following are characteristics of connective tissue? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Contains extracellular matrix
  • B) Avascular
  • C) Provides structural support
  • D) High regeneration capacity
Answer: A, C
Explanation: Connective tissue has an extracellular matrix and provides structural support. It may or may not be vascular, and its regenerative capacity varies.

10. True or False: Smooth muscle lacks striations and is found in the walls of hollow organs.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Smooth muscle is non-striated and contracts involuntarily to control movements in hollow organs like the stomach and intestines.

11. Which connective tissue type stores energy and provides insulation?

  • A) Loose Areolar
  • B) Adipose Tissue
  • C) Dense Regular
  • D) Fibrocartilage
Answer: B) Adipose Tissue
Explanation: Adipose tissue stores fat for energy, provides insulation, and cushions organs.

12. Which of the following are functions of epithelial tissue? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Protection
  • B) Absorption
  • C) Secretion
  • D) Conducting electrical impulses
Answer: A, B, C
Explanation: Epithelial tissue serves functions such as protection, absorption, and secretion. Conducting electrical impulses is a function of nervous tissue.

13. True or False: Cartilage is a vascular connective tissue.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Cartilage is avascular, meaning it lacks blood vessels and receives nutrients through diffusion.

14. Which type of connective tissue forms the intervertebral discs?

  • A) Hyaline Cartilage
  • B) Elastic Cartilage
  • C) Fibrocartilage
  • D) Loose Areolar
Answer: C) Fibrocartilage
Explanation: Fibrocartilage provides strong support and absorbs compressive forces in intervertebral discs.

15. Which tissue type contains goblet cells that secrete mucus?

  • A) Simple Cuboidal Epithelium
  • B) Pseudostratified Columnar Epithelium
  • C) Simple Squamous Epithelium
  • D) Transitional Epithelium
Answer: B) Pseudostratified Columnar Epithelium
Explanation: Pseudostratified columnar epithelium often contains goblet cells that secrete mucus to trap particles and protect surfaces.

16. True or False: Reticular connective tissue forms the framework for organs such as the spleen and lymph nodes.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Reticular connective tissue provides structural support to soft organs like the spleen and lymph nodes.

17. Which of the following epithelial tissues is found in the urinary bladder and allows for stretching?

  • A) Simple Squamous
  • B) Transitional
  • C) Stratified Cuboidal
  • D) Pseudostratified Columnar
Answer: B) Transitional
Explanation: Transitional epithelium is specialized for stretching and recoiling, making it ideal for the urinary bladder.

18. Which connective tissue contains a fluid matrix called plasma?

  • A) Bone
  • B) Blood
  • C) Cartilage
  • D) Loose Areolar
Answer: B) Blood
Explanation: Blood is a connective tissue with a fluid matrix (plasma) that transports nutrients, oxygen, and waste.

19. True or False: Skeletal muscle fibers are multinucleated.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Skeletal muscle fibers are long and multinucleated, allowing for efficient protein synthesis and contraction.

20. Which of the following are characteristics of cardiac muscle? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Striated
  • B) Multinucleated
  • C) Involuntary
  • D) Contains intercalated discs
Answer: A, C, D
Explanation: Cardiac muscle is striated, contracts involuntarily, and contains intercalated discs for synchronized contraction. It is typically uninucleated.

21. True or False: Simple cuboidal epithelium is primarily involved in secretion and absorption.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Simple cuboidal epithelium is found in glands and kidney tubules, specializing in secretion and absorption.

22. Which connective tissue type resists compression and is found in the knee and intervertebral discs?

  • A) Hyaline Cartilage
  • B) Elastic Cartilage
  • C) Fibrocartilage
  • D) Dense Regular Connective Tissue
Answer: C) Fibrocartilage
Explanation: Fibrocartilage resists compression and provides strong support in high-stress areas like intervertebral discs and knees.

23. True or False: Epithelial tissue has a high regeneration capacity.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Epithelial tissue regenerates rapidly due to its proximity to the vascular connective tissue below.

24. What is the function of gap junctions in cardiac muscle?

  • A) Prevent leakage between cells
  • B) Allow ions to flow between cells for synchronized contraction
  • C) Provide structural support
  • D) Transport oxygen
Answer: B) Allow ions to flow between cells for synchronized contraction
Explanation: Gap junctions facilitate ion flow, enabling synchronized contraction of cardiac muscle.

25. Which type of epithelial tissue lines the digestive tract and may contain goblet cells?

  • A) Simple Columnar
  • B) Simple Squamous
  • C) Transitional
  • D) Stratified Cuboidal
Answer: A) Simple Columnar
Explanation: Simple columnar epithelium lines the digestive tract and often contains goblet cells for mucus secretion.

26. True or False: Bone tissue is highly vascularized.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Bone tissue is highly vascularized, allowing it to heal faster than avascular tissues like cartilage.

27. Which of the following are functions of adipose tissue? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Energy storage
  • B) Protection
  • C) Insulation
  • D) Transporting oxygen
Answer: A, B, C
Explanation: Adipose tissue stores energy, provides cushioning for organs, and insulates the body against heat loss.

28. Which epithelial tissue is found in areas subjected to friction, such as the skin and esophagus?

  • A) Simple Squamous
  • B) Simple Cuboidal
  • C) Stratified Squamous
  • D) Transitional
Answer: C) Stratified Squamous
Explanation: Stratified squamous epithelium provides protection against abrasion in areas like the epidermis and esophagus.

29. True or False: The extracellular matrix of connective tissue is made of fibers and ground substance.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The extracellular matrix of connective tissue consists of protein fibers (e.g., collagen) and a ground substance that provides structural and functional support.

30. Which of the following are types of muscle tissue? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Skeletal
  • B) Smooth
  • C) Cardiac
  • D) Elastic
Answer: A, B, C
Explanation: The three types of muscle tissue are skeletal, smooth, and cardiac. Elastic is not a type of muscle tissue; it refers to connective tissue fibers.
