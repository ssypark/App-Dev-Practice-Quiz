Topic 2: Chemical Level of Organization Quiz

1. What is the basic unit of matter?

  • A) Molecule
  • B) Atom
  • C) Proton
  • D) Electron
Answer: B) Atom
Explanation: The atom is the fundamental unit of matter, consisting of protons, neutrons, and electrons.

2. True or False: Protons have a negative charge.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Protons carry a positive charge, while electrons are negatively charged.

3. Which of the following are macromolecules? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Proteins
  • B) Lipids
  • C) Water
  • D) Carbohydrates
  • E) Nucleic acids
Answer: A, B, D, E
Explanation: Proteins, lipids, carbohydrates, and nucleic acids are macromolecules. Water is not a macromolecule.

4. Which subatomic particle determines the identity of an element?

  • A) Proton
  • B) Neutron
  • C) Electron
  • D) Positron
Answer: A) Proton
Explanation: The number of protons, or atomic number, determines the identity of an element.

5. True or False: Ionic bonds are formed when atoms share electrons equally.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Ionic bonds form when one atom transfers electrons to another, while covalent bonds involve sharing electrons.

6. Which of the following are functions of proteins? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Providing energy
  • B) Acting as enzymes
  • C) Transporting molecules
  • D) Storing genetic information
Answer: A, B, C
Explanation: Proteins provide energy, act as enzymes, and transport molecules. Storing genetic information is a function of nucleic acids.

7. What type of bond forms between water molecules?

  • A) Ionic bonds
  • B) Covalent bonds
  • C) Hydrogen bonds
  • D) Metallic bonds
Answer: C) Hydrogen bonds
Explanation: Hydrogen bonds form between the slightly positive hydrogen atom of one water molecule and the slightly negative oxygen atom of another.

8. True or False: Lipids are hydrophilic and dissolve easily in water.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Lipids are hydrophobic, meaning they repel water and do not dissolve easily in it.

9. Which macromolecule is primarily responsible for storing genetic information?

  • A) Proteins
  • B) Lipids
  • C) Nucleic acids
  • D) Carbohydrates
Answer: C) Nucleic acids
Explanation: Nucleic acids like DNA and RNA store and transmit genetic information.

10. Which of the following are characteristics of water? (Select all that apply)

  • A) High heat capacity
  • B) Universal solvent
  • C) Nonpolar
  • D) Cohesion and adhesion
Answer: A, B, D
Explanation: Water has a high heat capacity, acts as a universal solvent, and exhibits cohesion and adhesion due to hydrogen bonding. It is polar, not nonpolar.

11. True or False: Enzymes speed up chemical reactions by lowering activation energy.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Enzymes are biological catalysts that reduce the activation energy needed for reactions, increasing their speed.

12. Which of the following are functions of carbohydrates? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Energy storage
  • B) Structural support
  • C) Catalysis of reactions
  • D) Cell signaling
Answer: A, B, D
Explanation: Carbohydrates store energy, provide structural support (e.g., cellulose), and participate in cell signaling. Catalysis is a function of proteins.

13. True or False: DNA and RNA are both double-stranded molecules.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: DNA is double-stranded, while RNA is single-stranded.

14. What is the primary function of ATP (adenosine triphosphate) in cells?

  • A) Providing structural support
  • B) Storing genetic information
  • C) Catalyzing reactions
  • D) Providing energy
Answer: D) Providing energy
Explanation: ATP is the primary energy currency of the cell, powering various cellular processes.

15. True or False: Lipids are the primary component of cell membranes.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Phospholipids, a type of lipid, form the bilayer of cell membranes, providing structure and regulating permeability.

16. Which type of lipid is the primary component of cell membranes?

  • A) Triglycerides
  • B) Phospholipids
  • C) Steroids
  • D) Saturated fats
Answer: B) Phospholipids
Explanation: Phospholipids form the bilayer of cell membranes, with hydrophilic heads and hydrophobic tails.

17. True or False: A dehydration synthesis reaction removes water to form bonds between molecules.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: In dehydration synthesis, water is removed to form covalent bonds between monomers, creating polymers.

18. Which of the following are examples of nucleotides? (Select all that apply)

  • A) ATP
  • B) DNA
  • C) RNA
  • D) GTP
Answer: A, D
Explanation: ATP (adenosine triphosphate) and GTP (guanosine triphosphate) are nucleotides. DNA and RNA are nucleic acids made of nucleotides.

19. True or False: Proteins are composed of amino acids joined by peptide bonds.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Proteins are polymers of amino acids linked by peptide bonds, forming polypeptides.

20. Which of the following are characteristics of enzymes? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Speed up chemical reactions
  • B) Are consumed in reactions
  • C) Lower activation energy
  • D) Have specific active sites
Answer: A, C, D
Explanation: Enzymes act as biological catalysts by lowering activation energy and are not consumed in reactions. They have specific active sites for substrates.

21. True or False: Covalent bonds are stronger than hydrogen bonds.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Covalent bonds involve the sharing of electrons and are stronger than hydrogen bonds, which are weaker attractions between molecules.

22. Which of the following are roles of water in the body? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Solvent for chemical reactions
  • B) Temperature regulation
  • C) Providing energy
  • D) Lubrication for joints
Answer: A, B, D
Explanation: Water acts as a solvent, regulates temperature, and lubricates joints. It does not provide energy.

23. True or False: A hydrolysis reaction uses water to break bonds in polymers.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Hydrolysis reactions involve the addition of water to break covalent bonds, splitting polymers into monomers.

24. Which macromolecule stores the most energy per gram?

  • A) Carbohydrates
  • B) Proteins
  • C) Lipids
  • D) Nucleic acids
Answer: C) Lipids
Explanation: Lipids, such as fats, store more energy per gram than carbohydrates or proteins.

25. True or False: Buffer systems help maintain pH balance in the body.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Buffers resist changes in pH by neutralizing excess acids or bases, helping to maintain homeostasis.

26. What type of reaction combines smaller molecules into larger ones?

  • A) Hydrolysis
  • B) Catabolic reaction
  • C) Dehydration synthesis
  • D) Exothermic reaction
Answer: C) Dehydration synthesis
Explanation: Dehydration synthesis combines smaller molecules into larger ones by removing water molecules.

27. Which macromolecule forms the genetic blueprint of life?

  • A) Protein
  • B) DNA
  • C) Lipid
  • D) Carbohydrate
Answer: B) DNA
Explanation: DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) carries genetic instructions for the development and function of living organisms.

28. True or False: Saturated fats have double bonds in their fatty acid chains.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Saturated fats lack double bonds, while unsaturated fats contain one or more double bonds.

29. Which of the following are examples of polysaccharides? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Starch
  • B) Glycogen
  • C) Glucose
  • D) Cellulose
Answer: A, B, D
Explanation: Starch, glycogen, and cellulose are polysaccharides. Glucose is a monosaccharide.

30. True or False: An acid is a substance that donates protons (H+) in a solution.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Acids increase the concentration of H+ ions in a solution, lowering the pH.
