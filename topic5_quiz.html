Topic 5: Integumentary System Quiz

1. What are the major organs included in the integumentary system?

  • A) Skin, hair, nails, glands, blood vessels, muscles, and nerves
  • B) Skin, bones, joints, glands, muscles, and nerves
  • C) Skin, glands, hair, and bones
  • D) Skin, tendons, nails, and nerves
Answer: A) Skin, hair, nails, glands, blood vessels, muscles, and nerves
Explanation: These components make up the integumentary system, which provides protection and other vital functions.

2. True or False: The skin is the largest organ of the body by weight.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The skin is the largest organ, covering about 2 square meters and weighing approximately 4.5-5kg (10-11 lbs).

3. What are the two major layers of the skin?

  • A) Epidermis and dermis
  • B) Epidermis and hypodermis
  • C) Dermis and hypodermis
  • D) Hypodermis and subcutaneous tissue
Answer: A) Epidermis and dermis
Explanation: The epidermis is the thinner outer layer, while the dermis is the thicker inner layer of the skin.

4. True or False: The hypodermis is considered part of the skin.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: The hypodermis, or subcutaneous layer, is not part of the skin but attaches it to underlying tissues and organs.

5. What are the five layers of the epidermis in thick skin?

  • A) Stratum basale, stratum lucidum, stratum granulosum, stratum spinosum, stratum corneum
  • B) Stratum basale, stratum spinosum, stratum granulosum, stratum corneum, stratum reticulare
  • C) Stratum spinosum, stratum lucidum, stratum granulosum, stratum corneum, stratum papillae
  • D) Stratum corneum, stratum lucidum, stratum granulosum, stratum reticulare, stratum basale
Answer: A) Stratum basale, stratum lucidum, stratum granulosum, stratum spinosum, stratum corneum
Explanation: These are the five layers of the epidermis in thick (hairless) skin found on palms and soles.

6. Which cells in the epidermis are responsible for producing melanin?

  • A) Keratinocytes
  • B) Melanocytes
  • C) Langerhans cells
  • D) Merkel cells
Answer: B) Melanocytes
Explanation: Melanocytes produce melanin, which protects the skin from UV damage and contributes to skin color.

7. Which layer of the skin contains blood vessels and nerves?

  • A) Epidermis
  • B) Dermis
  • C) Hypodermis
  • D) Stratum corneum
Answer: B) Dermis
Explanation: The dermis contains blood vessels, nerves, glands, and other structures that support the epidermis.

8. True or False: Keratinocytes are the most abundant cells in the epidermis.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Keratinocytes are the most abundant cells in the epidermis, producing keratin, a protein that provides strength and waterproofing.

9. What is the function of sebaceous glands?

  • A) Producing sweat
  • B) Producing sebum
  • C) Producing melanin
  • D) Producing vitamin D
Answer: B) Producing sebum
Explanation: Sebaceous glands secrete sebum, an oily substance that lubricates and waterproofs the skin and hair.

10. True or False: Apocrine sweat glands are active throughout life and are found all over the body.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Apocrine sweat glands are found in specific areas like the armpits and groin and become active during puberty.

11. What type of connective tissue is found in the dermis?

  • A) Dense irregular connective tissue
  • B) Loose connective tissue
  • C) Dense regular connective tissue
  • D) Elastic connective tissue
Answer: A) Dense irregular connective tissue
Explanation: The dermis consists primarily of dense irregular connective tissue, providing strength and flexibility to the skin.

12. True or False: Melanin production increases in response to UV radiation.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Melanocytes increase melanin production when exposed to UV radiation to protect the skin from DNA damage.

13. Which layer of the epidermis is responsible for cell division and renewal?

  • A) Stratum corneum
  • B) Stratum lucidum
  • C) Stratum spinosum
  • D) Stratum basale
Answer: D) Stratum basale
Explanation: The stratum basale is the deepest layer of the epidermis, where cells actively divide and renew the skin.

14. What type of sweat gland plays a role in thermoregulation?

  • A) Apocrine glands
  • B) Sebaceous glands
  • C) Eccrine glands
  • D) Ceruminous glands
Answer: C) Eccrine glands
Explanation: Eccrine glands produce sweat that evaporates to cool the body, playing a critical role in thermoregulation.

15. True or False: The integumentary system produces vitamin D when exposed to sunlight.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The skin synthesizes vitamin D when exposed to UV radiation, which is essential for calcium absorption and bone health.

16. What is the primary function of the arrector pili muscle?

  • A) To produce sebum
  • B) To raise the hair during cold or emotional states
  • C) To regulate sweat production
  • D) To transport melanin to keratinocytes
Answer: B) To raise the hair during cold or emotional states
Explanation: The arrector pili muscle contracts, causing hair to stand on end, often referred to as "goosebumps."

17. True or False: The epidermis is avascular and relies on the dermis for nutrient supply.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The epidermis lacks blood vessels (avascular) and relies on diffusion of nutrients from the dermis.

18. Which layer of the epidermis contains cells filled with keratohyalin granules?

  • A) Stratum corneum
  • B) Stratum granulosum
  • C) Stratum spinosum
  • D) Stratum basale
Answer: B) Stratum granulosum
Explanation: The stratum granulosum contains cells filled with keratohyalin granules, which are essential for forming the tough outer layer of the skin.

19. True or False: Ceruminous glands are modified sweat glands that produce earwax.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Ceruminous glands produce cerumen (earwax), which protects the ear canal from foreign particles and microorganisms.

20. Which pigment contributes to skin color and is found in the dermis?

  • A) Melanin
  • B) Carotene
  • C) Hemoglobin
  • D) All of the above
Answer: D) All of the above
Explanation: Skin color is influenced by melanin (produced by melanocytes), carotene (a yellow-orange pigment), and hemoglobin (red pigment in blood).

21. What is the outermost layer of the epidermis?

  • A) Stratum lucidum
  • B) Stratum spinosum
  • C) Stratum basale
  • D) Stratum corneum
Answer: D) Stratum corneum
Explanation: The stratum corneum is the outermost layer, composed of dead keratinized cells that form a tough protective barrier.

22. True or False: Hair and nails are composed of keratinized cells.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Both hair and nails are made of keratinized cells, providing strength and durability.

23. Which type of burn affects the epidermis, dermis, and subcutaneous tissue?

  • A) First-degree burn
  • B) Second-degree burn
  • C) Third-degree burn
  • D) Partial-thickness burn
Answer: C) Third-degree burn
Explanation: Third-degree burns destroy the epidermis, dermis, and extend into the subcutaneous tissue, often requiring medical intervention.

24. True or False: The papillary region of the dermis contains dermal papillae that form fingerprints.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The dermal papillae in the papillary region form ridges on the skin surface, resulting in fingerprints.

25. Which phase of hair growth involves active growth?

  • A) Anagen
  • B) Catagen
  • C) Telogen
  • D) Resting
Answer: A) Anagen
Explanation: The anagen phase is the active growth phase of the hair cycle, lasting several years.

26. True or False: Langerhans cells in the epidermis are involved in the immune response.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Langerhans cells are antigen-presenting cells in the epidermis that help the immune system detect and respond to pathogens.

27. Which skin structure is responsible for sensation?

  • A) Sebaceous glands
  • B) Sensory receptors
  • C) Arrector pili muscles
  • D) Melanocytes
Answer: B) Sensory receptors
Explanation: Sensory receptors in the skin detect touch, pressure, pain, and temperature.

28. What is the medical term for hair loss?

  • A) Alopecia
  • B) Hirsutism
  • C) Hypertrichosis
  • D) Hypotrichosis
Answer: A) Alopecia
Explanation: Alopecia refers to hair loss, which can occur for various reasons, including genetics and medical conditions.

29. True or False: The skin acts as a barrier to water loss and microbial invasion.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The skin's keratinized outer layer and acidic pH form a protective barrier against water loss and pathogens.

30. What protein in the skin provides waterproofing and strength?

  • A) Collagen
  • B) Elastin
  • C) Keratin
  • D) Melanin
Answer: C) Keratin
Explanation: Keratin is a structural protein that strengthens the skin and provides a waterproof barrier.
