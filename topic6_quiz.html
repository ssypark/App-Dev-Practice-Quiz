Topic 6: Bone Tissue Quiz

1. What are the functions of the skeletal system? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Support
  • B) Protection
  • C) Triglyceride storage
  • D) Blood cell production
Answer: A, B, C, D
Explanation: The skeletal system provides support, protects organs, stores triglycerides, and produces blood cells.

2. True or False: The epiphysis is the shaft of a long bone.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: The epiphysis is the end of a long bone, while the shaft is called the diaphysis.

3. What is the structural unit of compact bone?

  • A) Trabeculae
  • B) Osteons
  • C) Lamellae
  • D) Osteocytes
Answer: B) Osteons
Explanation: Osteons, also known as Haversian systems, are the structural units of compact bone.

4. True or False: Spongy bone contains osteons.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Spongy bone does not contain osteons. It consists of trabeculae, which form a lattice-like structure.

5. What type of bone cell is responsible for bone resorption?

  • A) Osteoblast
  • B) Osteocyte
  • C) Osteoclast
  • D) Osteoprogenitor
Answer: C) Osteoclast
Explanation: Osteoclasts are responsible for bone resorption, breaking down bone tissue to release minerals.

6. Which layer of the bone contains the medullary cavity?

  • A) Epiphysis
  • B) Metaphysis
  • C) Diaphysis
  • D) Periosteum
Answer: C) Diaphysis
Explanation: The diaphysis (shaft) of a long bone contains the medullary cavity, which stores yellow bone marrow in adults.

7. True or False: Red bone marrow is primarily responsible for storing triglycerides.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Red bone marrow is responsible for blood cell production, while yellow bone marrow stores triglycerides.

8. Which hormone is responsible for increasing blood calcium levels by stimulating bone resorption?

  • A) Calcitonin
  • B) Parathyroid hormone (PTH)
  • C) Growth hormone
  • D) Vitamin D
Answer: B) Parathyroid hormone (PTH)
Explanation: PTH increases blood calcium levels by stimulating osteoclast activity and promoting bone resorption.

9. True or False: Compact bone is found in the interior of bones, providing structural support.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Spongy bone is found in the interior of bones, while compact bone forms the outer layer.

10. What is the role of calcitonin in bone homeostasis?

  • A) Stimulates bone resorption
  • B) Increases osteoblast activity
  • C) Promotes calcium absorption in the intestines
  • D) Increases osteoclast activity
Answer: B) Increases osteoblast activity
Explanation: Calcitonin reduces blood calcium levels by stimulating osteoblasts to deposit calcium into the bone.

11. Which type of bone cell is derived from monocytes?

  • A) Osteoblast
  • B) Osteocyte
  • C) Osteoclast
  • D) Osteoprogenitor
Answer: C) Osteoclast
Explanation: Osteoclasts are derived from monocytes, a type of white blood cell, and are responsible for bone resorption.

12. True or False: The periosteum contains osteoblasts and osteoclasts.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The periosteum, a connective tissue layer covering the bone, contains osteoblasts and osteoclasts that aid in bone growth and repair.

13. What is the name of the small channels that connect osteocytes in compact bone?

  • A) Lacunae
  • B) Canaliculi
  • C) Lamellae
  • D) Haversian canals
Answer: B) Canaliculi
Explanation: Canaliculi are small channels that connect osteocytes, allowing for the exchange of nutrients and waste.

14. Which region of the long bone contains the growth plate?

  • A) Diaphysis
  • B) Metaphysis
  • C) Epiphysis
  • D) Medullary cavity
Answer: B) Metaphysis
Explanation: The metaphysis contains the epiphyseal growth plate, which is responsible for longitudinal bone growth in children.

15. True or False: Trabeculae are found in compact bone.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Trabeculae are found in spongy bone and provide structural support within the bone’s interior.

16. Which type of bone formation occurs directly within connective tissue membranes?

  • A) Endochondral ossification
  • B) Intramembranous ossification
  • C) Compact bone formation
  • D) Appositional growth
Answer: B) Intramembranous ossification
Explanation: Intramembranous ossification is the process by which flat bones, such as the skull, are formed directly within mesenchymal connective tissue.

17. True or False: Endochondral ossification requires a cartilage model to form bone.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Endochondral ossification begins with a hyaline cartilage model, which is gradually replaced by bone.

18. What is the name of the process by which bones grow in thickness?

  • A) Longitudinal growth
  • B) Appositional growth
  • C) Endochondral growth
  • D) Intramembranous growth
Answer: B) Appositional growth
Explanation: Appositional growth allows bones to grow in thickness by adding new layers of bone tissue to the surface.

19. True or False: Vitamin D is required for proper calcium absorption in the intestines.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Vitamin D enhances calcium absorption in the intestines, which is essential for bone health and mineralization.

20. What is the name of the condition characterized by reduced bone mass and increased fragility?

  • A) Osteomalacia
  • B) Osteoarthritis
  • C) Osteoporosis
  • D) Rickets
Answer: C) Osteoporosis
Explanation: Osteoporosis is a condition in which bones lose mass, becoming weak and more prone to fractures.

21. True or False: Bones undergo constant remodeling throughout life.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Bone remodeling is a continuous process involving osteoclasts (bone resorption) and osteoblasts (bone formation).

22. Which bone cells are primarily involved in bone formation?

  • A) Osteoblasts
  • B) Osteoclasts
  • C) Osteocytes
  • D) Chondrocytes
Answer: A) Osteoblasts
Explanation: Osteoblasts are responsible for synthesizing new bone matrix and initiating bone mineralization.

23. What is the name of the connective tissue that lines the medullary cavity?

  • A) Periosteum
  • B) Endosteum
  • C) Epiphyseal plate
  • D) Cartilage
Answer: B) Endosteum
Explanation: The endosteum is a thin connective tissue lining the medullary cavity, involved in bone growth and remodeling.

24. True or False: Rickets is caused by a deficiency of vitamin D in adults.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Rickets is caused by vitamin D deficiency in children, leading to soft and deformed bones. In adults, this condition is called osteomalacia.

25. What is the role of the Haversian canal in compact bone?

  • A) Stores calcium
  • B) Provides attachment points for muscles
  • C) Contains blood vessels and nerves
  • D) Houses osteocytes
Answer: C) Contains blood vessels and nerves
Explanation: The Haversian canal runs through the center of osteons, allowing blood vessels and nerves to supply bone tissue.

26. True or False: The axial skeleton includes the limbs and girdles.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: The axial skeleton includes the skull, vertebral column, and ribcage. The appendicular skeleton includes the limbs and girdles.

27. Which vitamin is essential for collagen synthesis in bone tissue?

  • A) Vitamin A
  • B) Vitamin C
  • C) Vitamin D
  • D) Vitamin K
Answer: B) Vitamin C
Explanation: Vitamin C is essential for collagen synthesis, which provides strength and flexibility to bone tissue.

28. True or False: Compact bone is more porous than spongy bone.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Spongy bone is more porous than compact bone, as it contains a lattice-like structure of trabeculae.

29. Which of the following bones is classified as a flat bone?

  • A) Femur
  • B) Humerus
  • C) Sternum
  • D) Patella
Answer: C) Sternum
Explanation: The sternum is classified as a flat bone, which provides protection and a surface for muscle attachment.

30. What is the function of the epiphyseal plate in growing bones?

  • A) Bone resorption
  • B) Bone remodeling
  • C) Lengthwise growth
  • D) Widthwise growth
Answer: C) Lengthwise growth
Explanation: The epiphyseal plate, or growth plate, is responsible for the lengthwise growth of long bones in children and adolescents.
