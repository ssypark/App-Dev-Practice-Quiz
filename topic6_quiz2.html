Topic 6: Bone Tissue Quiz (Part 2)

1. True or False: Bone is a type of connective tissue.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Bone is a specialized connective tissue composed of cells and extracellular matrix that supports and protects the body.

2. What are the two types of bone tissue?

  • A) Compact and cancellous bone
  • B) Cortical and trabecular bone
  • C) Spongy and compact bone
  • D) All of the above
Answer: D) All of the above
Explanation: The terms compact, cortical, spongy, and cancellous are used to describe the two main types of bone tissue.

3. Which bone cell is responsible for forming new bone tissue?

  • A) Osteocytes
  • B) Osteoblasts
  • C) Osteoclasts
  • D) Chondrocytes
Answer: B) Osteoblasts
Explanation: Osteoblasts are bone-forming cells that secrete the matrix and aid in mineralizing bone.

4. True or False: The periosteum covers the outer surface of bones and is involved in bone growth and repair.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The periosteum is a fibrous membrane that contains cells essential for bone growth and repair.

5. What is the primary role of osteoclasts in bone tissue?

  • A) Bone formation
  • B) Bone resorption
  • C) Calcium storage
  • D) Cartilage production
Answer: B) Bone resorption
Explanation: Osteoclasts break down bone tissue during bone remodeling, releasing calcium and other minerals into the bloodstream.

6. True or False: The diaphysis of a long bone is filled with red bone marrow in adults.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: In adults, the diaphysis of long bones contains yellow bone marrow, which stores triglycerides, while red bone marrow is typically found in flat bones and the ends of long bones.

7. What is the primary mineral stored in bone tissue?

  • A) Potassium
  • B) Sodium
  • C) Calcium
  • D) Iron
Answer: C) Calcium
Explanation: Bone tissue stores calcium, which is essential for maintaining bone strength and other physiological functions.

8. True or False: Spongy bone is primarily located in the diaphysis of long bones.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Spongy bone is found in the epiphysis (ends) of long bones, while the diaphysis is composed mainly of compact bone.

9. Which of the following structures is found in an osteon?

  • A) Lamellae
  • B) Haversian canal
  • C) Canaliculi
  • D) All of the above
Answer: D) All of the above
Explanation: An osteon includes concentric lamellae, a central Haversian canal, and canaliculi that connect osteocytes within lacunae.

10. What type of cartilage is found at the ends of long bones in a joint?

  • A) Elastic cartilage
  • B) Fibrocartilage
  • C) Hyaline cartilage
  • D) Articular cartilage
Answer: D) Articular cartilage
Explanation: Articular cartilage is a type of hyaline cartilage that covers the ends of bones in synovial joints to reduce friction and absorb shock.

11. True or False: Endochondral ossification involves the replacement of cartilage with bone.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Endochondral ossification involves the gradual replacement of a cartilage model with bone tissue during fetal development and growth.

12. Which part of the bone contains the medullary cavity?

  • A) Epiphysis
  • B) Metaphysis
  • C) Diaphysis
  • D) Periosteum
Answer: C) Diaphysis
Explanation: The diaphysis, or shaft of a long bone, contains the medullary cavity, which stores yellow marrow in adults.

13. What is the function of trabeculae in spongy bone?

  • A) Storing fat
  • B) Providing strength while reducing weight
  • C) Storing calcium
  • D) Connecting muscles to bones
Answer: B) Providing strength while reducing weight
Explanation: Trabeculae in spongy bone create a lattice structure that provides strength without the weight of compact bone.

14. True or False: The epiphyseal plate is responsible for bone growth in width.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: The epiphyseal plate, or growth plate, is responsible for longitudinal growth in bones, not width.

15. Which type of bone cell becomes embedded in the matrix and helps maintain bone tissue?

  • A) Osteoclast
  • B) Osteoblast
  • C) Osteocyte
  • D) Chondrocyte
Answer: C) Osteocyte
Explanation: Osteocytes are mature bone cells that become embedded in the bone matrix and maintain bone tissue.

16. True or False: Compact bone is organized into osteons, while spongy bone is arranged in trabeculae.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Compact bone is composed of osteons for structural support, while spongy bone consists of trabeculae to reduce weight and distribute stress.

17. Which hormone stimulates bone resorption to increase blood calcium levels?

  • A) Calcitonin
  • B) Parathyroid hormone (PTH)
  • C) Growth hormone
  • D) Vitamin D
Answer: B) Parathyroid hormone (PTH)
Explanation: PTH stimulates osteoclast activity to break down bone and release calcium into the bloodstream.

18. What is the function of canaliculi in bone tissue?

  • A) To store calcium
  • B) To connect lacunae and allow communication between osteocytes
  • C) To supply blood to the bone matrix
  • D) To provide structural support
Answer: B) To connect lacunae and allow communication between osteocytes
Explanation: Canaliculi are small channels that connect lacunae, enabling nutrient and waste exchange between osteocytes.

19. True or False: Bones stop remodeling once growth is complete.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Bone remodeling is a continuous process throughout life, balancing bone resorption and formation to adapt to stress and maintain strength.

20. Which vitamin is essential for collagen synthesis in bone tissue?

  • A) Vitamin A
  • B) Vitamin C
  • C) Vitamin D
  • D) Vitamin K
Answer: B) Vitamin C
Explanation: Vitamin C is crucial for collagen synthesis, which provides flexibility and strength to bone tissue.

21. True or False: Osteoporosis is caused by an imbalance where bone resorption outpaces bone formation.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Osteoporosis occurs when bone resorption exceeds bone formation, leading to weakened and brittle bones.

22. What is the primary function of red bone marrow?

  • A) Storing fat
  • B) Producing blood cells
  • C) Storing calcium
  • D) Forming cartilage
Answer: B) Producing blood cells
Explanation: Red bone marrow produces red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets in a process called hematopoiesis.

23. Which hormone promotes calcium deposition in bones?

  • A) Calcitonin
  • B) Parathyroid hormone (PTH)
  • C) Estrogen
  • D) Thyroxine
Answer: A) Calcitonin
Explanation: Calcitonin inhibits osteoclast activity and promotes calcium deposition in bones, lowering blood calcium levels.

24. True or False: The axial skeleton includes the skull, vertebral column, and rib cage.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The axial skeleton provides structural support and protection for the brain, spinal cord, and thoracic organs.

25. What type of bone growth increases the width of bones?

  • A) Longitudinal growth
  • B) Appositional growth
  • C) Endochondral growth
  • D) Intramembranous growth
Answer: B) Appositional growth
Explanation: Appositional growth involves the addition of bone tissue to the outer surface, increasing bone width.

26. Which part of the bone stores yellow bone marrow in adults?

  • A) Epiphysis
  • B) Medullary cavity
  • C) Periosteum
  • D) Trabeculae
Answer: B) Medullary cavity
Explanation: The medullary cavity, located in the diaphysis of long bones, stores yellow bone marrow in adults.

27. True or False: Growth hormone promotes bone growth during childhood by stimulating the epiphyseal plate.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Growth hormone stimulates the activity of the epiphyseal plate, promoting bone growth during childhood and adolescence.

28. What is the primary component of the bone matrix?

  • A) Collagen fibers and calcium salts
  • B) Elastic fibers and potassium salts
  • C) Proteins and sodium salts
  • D) Adipose tissue and phosphate salts
Answer: A) Collagen fibers and calcium salts
Explanation: The bone matrix is primarily composed of collagen fibers for flexibility and calcium salts for strength.

29. Which vitamin enhances calcium absorption in the intestines?

  • A) Vitamin A
  • B) Vitamin C
  • C) Vitamin D
  • D) Vitamin K
Answer: C) Vitamin D
Explanation: Vitamin D increases calcium absorption in the intestines, supporting bone health and mineralization.

30. True or False: Osteoblasts are derived from osteoprogenitor cells.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Osteoblasts develop from osteoprogenitor cells, which differentiate into bone-forming cells.
