Topic 7: Muscular Tissue Quiz

1. What are the four properties of muscle tissue?

  • A) Excitability, Contractility, Extensibility, Elasticity
  • B) Strength, Flexibility, Durability, Elasticity
  • C) Extensibility, Excitability, Flexibility, Durability
  • D) Elasticity, Strength, Extensibility, Flexibility
Answer: A) Excitability, Contractility, Extensibility, Elasticity
Explanation: These are the defining properties that allow muscles to respond, contract, stretch, and return to their original length.

2. True or False: Myofibrils extend the entire length of a muscle fiber.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Myofibrils are thread-like structures that run the length of the muscle fiber, containing the contractile units (sarcomeres).

3. What are the two types of microfilaments within myofibrils?

  • A) Actin and Myosin
  • B) Actin and Tropomyosin
  • C) Myosin and Troponin
  • D) Tropomyosin and Troponin
Answer: A) Actin and Myosin
Explanation: Actin (thin filament) and Myosin (thick filament) interact to facilitate muscle contraction.

4. Which connective tissue layer surrounds a muscle fiber?

  • A) Epimysium
  • B) Perimysium
  • C) Endomysium
  • D) Sarcolemma
Answer: C) Endomysium
Explanation: The endomysium is a thin layer of connective tissue that wraps around each individual muscle fiber.

5. True or False: A tendon connects a muscle to a bone.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Tendons are strong bands of connective tissue that connect muscles to bones.

6. Which type of respiration produces the most ATP?

  • A) Anaerobic respiration
  • B) Aerobic respiration
  • C) Glycolysis
  • D) Fermentation
Answer: B) Aerobic respiration
Explanation: Aerobic respiration requires oxygen and produces up to 38 ATP per glucose molecule, making it more efficient than anaerobic processes.

7. Which ion is essential for muscle contraction?

  • A) Sodium (Na+)
  • B) Calcium (Ca2+)
  • C) Potassium (K+)
  • D) Magnesium (Mg2+)
Answer: B) Calcium (Ca2+)
Explanation: Calcium ions are released from the sarcoplasmic reticulum and bind to troponin, initiating muscle contraction.

8. True or False: The sarcomere is the smallest functional unit of a muscle fiber.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The sarcomere is the repeating structural and functional unit within a myofibril, crucial for muscle contraction.

9. Which of the following are phases of a muscle twitch? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Latent period
  • B) Contraction period
  • C) Relaxation period
  • D) Recovery period
Answer: A, B, C
Explanation: The phases of a muscle twitch are the latent period (delay before contraction), contraction period, and relaxation period.

10. What is the role of ATP in muscle contraction?

  • A) Powers the detachment of myosin heads
  • B) Allows calcium release from the sarcoplasmic reticulum
  • C) Both A and B
  • D) None of the above
Answer: C) Both A and B
Explanation: ATP is crucial for detaching myosin heads from actin and powering the calcium pump to restore ion concentrations.

11. True or False: Smooth muscle fibers are striated.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Smooth muscle fibers lack striations and have a spindle-shaped appearance, unlike skeletal and cardiac muscle.

12. Which type of muscle is involuntary and found in the walls of hollow organs?

  • A) Skeletal muscle
  • B) Cardiac muscle
  • C) Smooth muscle
  • D) All of the above
Answer: C) Smooth muscle
Explanation: Smooth muscle is involuntary and lines the walls of hollow organs such as blood vessels and the digestive tract.

13. True or False: Cardiac muscle fibers are connected by intercalated discs.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Intercalated discs are specialized connections between cardiac muscle cells that allow for synchronized contraction.

14. Which of the following proteins regulate muscle contraction? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Troponin
  • B) Tropomyosin
  • C) Actin
  • D) Myosin
Answer: A, B
Explanation: Troponin and tropomyosin regulate muscle contraction by controlling the interaction of actin and myosin.

15. True or False: Fast glycolytic fibers fatigue more quickly than slow oxidative fibers.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Fast glycolytic fibers generate rapid, powerful contractions but fatigue quickly compared to slow oxidative fibers.

16. What is the role of the sarcoplasmic reticulum in muscle contraction?

  • A) Storing oxygen
  • B) Producing ATP
  • C) Storing and releasing calcium ions
  • D) Breaking down acetylcholine
Answer: C) Storing and releasing calcium ions
Explanation: The sarcoplasmic reticulum stores calcium ions and releases them during muscle contraction to initiate the interaction of actin and myosin.

17. True or False: Skeletal muscle fibers are multinucleated.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: Skeletal muscle fibers are long, cylindrical, and multinucleated, allowing them to support their extensive protein synthesis requirements.

18. Which type of muscle fiber is best suited for endurance activities?

  • A) Fast oxidative fibers
  • B) Fast glycolytic fibers
  • C) Slow oxidative fibers
  • D) Intermediate fibers
Answer: C) Slow oxidative fibers
Explanation: Slow oxidative fibers rely on aerobic respiration, resist fatigue, and are best suited for endurance activities like long-distance running.

19. True or False: The Z-line defines the boundary of a sarcomere.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The Z-line anchors thin filaments and defines the boundaries of a sarcomere, the functional unit of a myofibril.

20. Which neurotransmitter is involved in skeletal muscle contraction?

  • A) Dopamine
  • B) Acetylcholine
  • C) Serotonin
  • D) Glutamate
Answer: B) Acetylcholine
Explanation: Acetylcholine is released at neuromuscular junctions, triggering muscle contraction by depolarizing the muscle fiber membrane.

21. True or False: The H-zone contains only thick filaments.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The H-zone is the central part of a sarcomere that contains only thick filaments (myosin) and disappears during complete contraction.

22. Which of the following are characteristics of cardiac muscle fibers? (Select all that apply)

  • A) Striated
  • B) Multinucleated
  • C) Intercalated discs
  • D) Involuntary
Answer: A, C, D
Explanation: Cardiac muscle fibers are striated, contain intercalated discs for synchronized contraction, and are involuntary. They are not multinucleated.

23. True or False: Muscle fatigue occurs when ATP supply exceeds demand.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Muscle fatigue occurs when ATP demand exceeds supply, leading to the accumulation of metabolic byproducts like lactic acid.

24. What is the primary function of myoglobin in muscle fibers?

  • A) Transporting calcium ions
  • B) Binding and storing oxygen
  • C) Breaking down glycogen
  • D) Generating ATP
Answer: B) Binding and storing oxygen
Explanation: Myoglobin stores oxygen within muscle fibers, providing a ready supply for aerobic respiration.

25. True or False: Muscle hypertrophy results from an increase in the number of muscle fibers.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: B) False
Explanation: Muscle hypertrophy occurs due to an increase in the size of muscle fibers, not their number.

26. Which structure conducts action potentials into the muscle fiber?

  • A) Sarcolemma
  • B) T-tubules
  • C) Sarcomere
  • D) Endomysium
Answer: B) T-tubules
Explanation: T-tubules (transverse tubules) conduct action potentials deep into the muscle fiber to trigger calcium release.

27. True or False: A motor unit consists of a motor neuron and the muscle fibers it innervates.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: A motor unit includes a motor neuron and all the muscle fibers it controls, enabling coordinated contraction.

28. Which term describes the increase in muscle tension without a change in length?

  • A) Isotonic contraction
  • B) Isometric contraction
  • C) Concentric contraction
  • D) Eccentric contraction
Answer: B) Isometric contraction
Explanation: In isometric contraction, the muscle generates tension without changing its length.

29. Which type of connective tissue surrounds fascicles?

  • A) Epimysium
  • B) Perimysium
  • C) Endomysium
  • D) Sarcolemma
Answer: B) Perimysium
Explanation: The perimysium is a layer of connective tissue that surrounds and separates fascicles (bundles of muscle fibers).

30. True or False: The I-band contains only thin filaments.

  • A) True
  • B) False
Answer: A) True
Explanation: The I-band contains only thin filaments (actin) and shortens during muscle contraction.
